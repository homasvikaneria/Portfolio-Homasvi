Built for Problems That Don’t Come Easy

I got into software to understand how systems actually work—not just to build features, but to see how data flows, how services scale, and where things break under real constraints.

I spend most of my time working on backend-heavy systems, designing APIs, handling authentication and access control, and thinking through how to structure applications so they’re reliable beyond just local environments. I’m comfortable working across the stack, but I’m most interested in what happens behind the scenes—how decisions at the system level affect performance, security, and scalability.

Lately, I’ve been pushing deeper into DevOps and cloud—learning how to containerize applications, manage deployments, and build systems that are closer to production than prototypes.

I’m looking for environments where the technical bar is high, the problems are real, and there’s room to build things that actually hold up under scale.
